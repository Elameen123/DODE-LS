Components of the hydraulic lift

HYDRAULIC CYLINDER

The hydraulic cylinder is a mechanical actuator that converts hydraulic pressure into linear motion.

It consists of a cylindrical tube (barrel), a piston, and a rod.

The hydraulic fluid from the pump enters one side of the cylinder, applying pressure to the piston.

HYDRAULIC PUMP

The hydraulic lift system has a hydraulic pump that pressurizes the hydraulic fluid.

This pump creates a flow of pressurized fluid, which is sent to the hydraulic cylinder.

ROLLERS ON TRACK

Rollers can be incorporated into guide rails or tracks along which the lift platform to move vertically.

These rollers help maintain stability and smooth movement,

PRESSURE HOSES

Hydraulic hoses serve as conduits for the flow of hydraulic fluid.

They connect various components of the hydraulic system, such as the hydraulic pump, control valves, hydraulic cylinders,

and the reservoir, allowing hydraulic fluid to be transported between these parts.

CONTROL PANEL

The control panel provides controls for lifting and lowering the platform or load.

These buttons or switches allow operators to raise or lower the lift to the desired height.

The lift direction controls are usually in the form of up and down arrows or switches.

WHEELS

Many hydraulic lifts are designed to be mobile, allowing them to be easily moved around a workspace or facility.

Wheels attached to the base of the lift provide the necessary mobility.
